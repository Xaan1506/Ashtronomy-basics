Mercury : The Swift Planet
Position in Solar System: 1st planet from the Sun. Size: Smallest planet in the solar system, slightly larger than our Moon. Orbit & Day: Mercury orbits the Sun in just 88 Earth days, but a single day (sunrise to sunrise) lasts about 176 Earth days. Surface: Rocky, cratered, and similar to the Moon. It has huge temperature swings from -173¬∞C at night to 427¬∞C during the day. Atmosphere: Almost no atmosphere, so it cannot retain heat. Earth : Our Home Planet
Position in Solar System: 3rd planet from the Sun. Size & Shape: The fifth-largest planet, slightly flattened at the poles (oblate spheroid). Orbit & Day: Takes 365.25 days to orbit the Sun; one day is 24 hours. Surface: About 71% covered by water, with continents and islands making up the rest. Atmosphere: A breathable mix of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), protecting life from harmful solar radiation. Life: The only known planet to support life, from microorganisms to humans. Interesting Fact: Earth has a natural satellite‚Äîthe Moon‚Äîwhich affects tides and stabilizes the planet‚Äôs rotation. Read more


Mars : The Red Planet
Position in Solar System: 4th planet from the Sun. Size & Surface: About half the size of Earth, with a reddish surface due to iron oxide (rust). Features include the largest volcano (Olympus Mons) and the deepest canyon (Valles Marineris). Orbit & Day: Takes 687 Earth days to orbit the Sun; one day lasts 24.6 hours. Atmosphere: Very thin, mostly carbon dioxide, so it cannot support human life without help. Moons: Has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos. Interesting Fact: Mars has signs of ancient water and is the primary target for future human exploration. Read more


Jupiter : The Gas Giant
Position in Solar System: 5th planet from the Sun. Size: Largest planet in the solar system, over 1,300 times the volume of Earth. Composition: A gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface. Atmosphere & Storms: Famous for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm larger than Earth, and colorful cloud bands. Moons: Has 95+ known moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system. Interesting Fact: Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and a faint ring system. Read more


Saturn : The Ringed Planet
Position in Solar System: 6th planet from the Sun. Size: Second-largest planet after Jupiter. Composition: A gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface. Rings: Famous for its stunning rings made of ice, rock, and dust. Moons: Has over 80 moons, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system. Interesting Fact: Saturn is so light that it would float in water if a large enough pool existed! Read more


Uranus : The Tilted Ice Giant
Position in Solar System: 7th planet from the Sun. Size: Third-largest planet by diameter. Composition: An ice giant made mostly of hydrogen, helium, and ices like water, ammonia, and methane. Unique Feature: Rotates on its side, with an extreme axial tilt of 98¬∞, making its poles experience 42 years of continuous sunlight or darkness. Rings & Moons: Has 13 faint rings and 27 known moons, named mostly after characters from literature. Interesting Fact: Its blue-green color comes from methane in the atmosphere, which absorbs red light. Read more


Neptune : The Windy Blue Planet
Position in Solar System: 8th planet from the Sun. Size: Fourth-largest planet by diameter. Composition: An ice giant made mostly of hydrogen, helium, and ices like water, ammonia, and methane. Atmosphere & Weather: Known for strongest winds in the solar system, reaching over 2,000 km/h. Moons & Rings: Has 14 known moons, including Triton, which orbits backward, and 6 faint rings. Interesting Fact: Neptune was the first planet discovered through mathematics before it was actually seen through a telescope. Read more

Pluto : The Dwarf Planet
Position in Solar System: Formerly the 9th planet; now classified as a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt. Size: Very small, about 1/6th the size of Earth's Moon. Surface & Atmosphere: Rocky and icy surface with mountains, plains, and frozen nitrogen. Has a thin atmosphere that expands when it comes closer to the Sun. Moons: Has 5 known moons, with Charon being the largest, almost half the size of Pluto itself. Orbit & Year: Takes 248 Earth years to orbit the Sun. Interesting Fact: Pluto was discovered in 1930 and reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006, sparking lots of debate. Read more
